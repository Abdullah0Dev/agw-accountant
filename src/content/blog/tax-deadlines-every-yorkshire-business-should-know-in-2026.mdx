# Tax Deadlines Every Yorkshire Business Should Know in 2026

Running a business comes with enough challenges without worrying about missed tax deadlines. Whether you're a sole trader, limited company, contractor or growing SME, keeping on top of your tax obligations is one of the simplest ways to avoid unnecessary penalties, protect your cash flow and keep your business running smoothly.

Many businesses only realise they've missed a deadline after receiving a fine. The good news? With a little planning and the right support, it's completely avoidable.

This guide covers the key UK tax deadlines every Yorkshire business owner should know in 2026.

---

## 1. Self Assessment Tax Return

If you're a sole trader, freelancer or company director who needs to complete a Self Assessment, you'll need to submit your tax return and pay any tax owed by the required deadline.

Missing this deadline can result in automatic penalties, with additional charges building up the longer the return remains outstanding.

### Top Tip

Keep your income and expenses organised throughout the year rather than leaving everything until January.

---

## 2. Corporation Tax

If you operate a limited company, you'll usually need to pay Corporation Tax on your profits.

Although your filing and payment deadlines depend on your company's accounting period, leaving it until the last minute can create unnecessary stress and cash flow pressure.

Planning ahead also gives you more opportunities to reduce your tax bill through legitimate business expenses and tax reliefs.

---

## 3. VAT Returns

If your business is VAT registered, you'll need to submit VAT returns and make payments on time.

Many businesses now use cloud accounting software to automate much of this process, reducing errors and saving valuable time.

Late submissions can result in penalties and interest, so it's worth keeping accurate records throughout the quarter.

---

## 4. PAYE and Payroll

If you employ staff, PAYE submissions and payments need to be made regularly.

Accurate payroll ensures your employees are paid correctly while keeping your business compliant with HMRC requirements.

Using payroll software or working with an accountant can significantly reduce administrative work.

---

## 5. Companies House Filing

Limited companies must also submit annual accounts and a confirmation statement to Companies House.

These filings keep your company legally compliant and ensure your public company information remains up to date.

Missing these deadlines can lead to financial penalties and, in serious cases, your company being struck off the register.

---

# How to Avoid Missing Deadlines

Successful businesses don't rely on memory—they build systems.

Here are five simple ways to stay on track:

- Keep digital records throughout the year.
- Use cloud accounting software such as Xero or QuickBooks.
- Set calendar reminders well in advance.
- Review your finances every month instead of once a year.
- Work with an accountant who monitors deadlines on your behalf.

A proactive approach gives you more time to focus on growing your business rather than worrying about paperwork.
