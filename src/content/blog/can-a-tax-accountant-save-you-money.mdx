A tax accountant can save you money by ensuring you're taking advantage of all eligible tax deductions and credits, and by helping you navigate complex tax laws. According to HMRC, *(Source: HMRC, 2026)*, small businesses and self-employed individuals who use a tax accountant are more likely to be compliant with tax regulations and less likely to face penalties. In fact, a study by the Federation of Small Businesses found that *(Source: FSB, 2025)*, 75% of small businesses that used a tax accountant reported a reduction in their tax liabilities.

### Why It Matters
The financial impact of not using a tax accountant can be significant. Without professional guidance, you may miss out on valuable tax savings or inadvertently trigger penalties for non-compliance. For example, *(Source: ACCA, 2026)*, the average small business in the UK wastes around £1,300 per year due to inefficient tax planning. By hiring a tax accountant, you can ensure you're maximizing your tax savings and minimizing your tax liabilities.

### Root Causes
So, why do so many small businesses and self-employed individuals struggle with tax planning? One major reason is the lack of a finance background, which can make it difficult to understand complex tax laws and regulations. Additionally, irregular income and changing HMRC rules can make it tough to stay on top of tax obligations. Time pressure and fear of getting it wrong can also lead to mistakes and missed opportunities for tax savings.
